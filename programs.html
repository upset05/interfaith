Programs & Activities

Seven Pillars of
Action & Impact

IPPAD translates faith-based principles into practical, structured, and measurable community interventions across Nigeria.

Our Work

What We Do

Each program pillar addresses an urgent community need — moving beyond dialogue into structured, measurable action.

01

Peacebuilding, Trauma Healing & Social Cohesion

Conflict mediation, trauma healing workshops, psychosocial support and counselling — especially for conflict-affected communities. Includes election violence prevention, community peace education, and interfaith youth engagement through school peace clubs.

Core Program
02

Health, Family & Wellbeing

Maternal, newborn, and child health advocacy. Family life and responsible parenting education, marriage and family counselling, and community-based support systems for holistic wellbeing.

Community Health
03

Education & Youth Development

Scholarships for vulnerable children. Support for internally displaced persons (IDPs) and out-of-school children. Youth empowerment initiatives and interfaith school peace clubs to build the next generation of peacebuilders.

Youth Focus
04

Economic Empowerment & Livelihoods

Vocational training, small business grants, and community-based livelihood development programs to address poverty and unemployment in vulnerable communities across Nigeria.

Economic Development
05

Advocacy, Justice & Rights

Legal awareness campaigns, advocacy for justice, equity, and inclusive governance. Countering hate speech and harmful narratives through faith-based messaging and structured community engagement.

Advocacy
06

Climate Action & Food Security

Climate awareness initiatives, environmental sustainability programs, and farmer support interventions. Promoting sustainable agricultural practices and community-based environmental protection.

Environment
07

Humanitarian & Emergency Response

Relief support for displaced persons, crisis response interventions, community resilience building, and correctional outreach including prison rehabilitation and reintegration support for inmates and ex-offenders.

Humanitarian
Strategic Objectives

15 Commitments to Action

1
Promote interfaith understanding and cooperation
2
Act as a formal body for consultation by statutory and other organisations seeking views of faith communities
3
Promote intra-faith and interfaith dialogue at all levels
4
Develop relationships with government, NGOs, statutory, private, and voluntary sectors
5
Promote the interests and concerns of all faith communities
6
Encourage joint social action to respond to opportunities and needs in communities
7
Provide representation in decision-making where joint faith community input is required
8
Promote, develop, and nurture leadership within faith communities and give voice to people of faith
9
Promote the health and well-being of communities
10
Inculcate social and religious awareness to eradicate hate crimes
11
Encourage all religions and denominations to stand together in fraternity and fellowship for peace
12
Provide opportunities for communication and relationship building between people of different faiths
13
Provide opportunities to celebrate the significant contributions of faith organisations to citizens' lives
14
Give awards to individuals who have distinguished themselves in peace and nation-building
15
Research, policy & documentation for data-driven, evidence-based peacebuilding advocacy
