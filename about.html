Who We Are

Faith as a Bridge,
Not a Barrier

The Imams & Pastors Interfaith Forum for Peace and Development (IPPAD) was born from a simple but profound conviction: that faith must serve as a bridge for unity, not a barrier to coexistence.

Founded by Bishop Dr. Sam Moses Tukura, JP — shaped by over two decades of experience in Zaria and Jos — IPPAD exists to transform interfaith relationships from passive coexistence into active collaboration for peace, development, and social transformation.

IPPAD emphasises coming together not just to talk, but to act collectively, build jointly, serve communities together, and generate measurable impact that transforms lives.

  • VisionA peaceful, cohesive society where Muslims and Christians live in mutual respect, promote peace-building, develop communities, feed the hungry, and build character, faith, and good values.
  • MissionTo foster a culture of mutual respect, trust, tolerance, and cooperation between Muslim and Christian leaders — leveraging shared moral values and partnerships for the betterment of society.
  • Core PurposeFrom conversation to collaboration, from dialogue to impact — IPPAD translates faith-based principles into practical, measurable community interventions.

Message from the Founder

Bishop Dr. Sam Moses Tukura, JP

Founder & Visioner, IPPAD

Having spent over two decades in Zaria and formative academic years in Jos — two cities that reflect both the beauty and the fragility of interfaith relations — I witnessed firsthand the gaps that often exist between communities that share the same spaces, yet struggle to fully trust, understand, and collaborate with one another.

My Master's research at the University of Jos focused on forgiveness and trauma within communities in Jos North Local Government Area — exploring pathways toward healing, reconciliation, and sustainable peace. This work reinforced the critical role that faith and community leadership can play in addressing deep-rooted divisions.

IPPAD was founded on shared values — with a clear commitment to faith-based peacebuilding, trauma healing, and community development. Today, IPPAD stands as a testament to what is possible when faith leaders rise above division and embrace a common mandate for humanity.

I invite partners, stakeholders, and all people of goodwill to join us. Together, we can transform faith into action — and action into lasting peace and sustainable development.

Governance

Leadership Structure

IPPAD operates under a transparent, dual-faith leadership structure ensuring interfaith balance and institutional accountability.

Founder

Bishop Dr. Sam Moses Tukura, JP — provides founding vision, strategic direction, and institutional leadership.

Co-Chairs

An Imam and a Pastor jointly lead the organisation — ensuring mutual respect, shared wisdom, and collective responsibility in all decisions.

Board of Trustees

Provides institutional oversight, governance, and fiduciary accountability for the organisation's operations and resources.

Advisory Council

Chaired by a retired Justice of the High Court of the FCT — providing strategic oversight, ethical guidance, and legal accountability.
